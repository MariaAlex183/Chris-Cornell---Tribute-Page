Chris Cornell

Life and Death

Christopher John Cornell was an American musician. He was best known as the lead vocalist, rhythm guitarist, and the primary lyricist for the rock bands Soundgarden and Audioslave. He also had a solo career and contributed to numerous movie soundtracks. Cornell was the founder and frontman of Temple of the Dog, a one-off tribute band dedicated to his late friend, musician Andrew Wood. Several music journalists, fan polls and fellow musicians have regarded Cornell as one of the greatest rock singers of all time.

Chris Cornell Biography

1964 - Christoper John Boyle was born on July 20 in Seattle, Washington.

1971 - He started to take piano lessons, having interest on Lynyrd Skynyrd, The Beatles and Alice Copper.

1978 - His parents divorced and he took his mother's last name "Cornell".

1979 - He became depressed due to his parents divorce, he left the piano and focused on the guitar and drums.

1980 - He left the high school, starting to work as a kitchen assistant.

1981 - His first band, in which he had the role of singer, was called The Jones Street Band and did covers of Rush, AC/DC, Sex Pistols and Ramones.

1982 - He moved out of his home and joined a band called The Shemps.

1984 - He formed Soundgarden in Illinois

1989 - He recorded his first album with Soundgarden titled "Louder than Love", which was nominated for the Grammy Awards.

1990 - He formed the band The Temple of Dog with Eddie Vedder as a way of tribute to his friend Andrew Wood. He got married with Susan Silver.

1991 - Soundgarden publishes "Badmotorfinger", showing sound solidity and high-level musical maturity, with clear influences from Led Zeppelin and The Stooges.

1994 - The album "Superunknown" was published, key for the grunge community and an essential title of the soundtrack of the 90's, featuring anthems like "Spoonman" and "Black Hole Sun". Doctors discovered that Cornell had severely strained his vocal cords. Soundgarden had to cancel several shows to prevent them from being permanently damaged.

1997 - Soundgarden was dissolved.

2000 - He was nominated for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance at the Grammy Awards for his single "Can't Change Me" from the album "Euphoria Morning", which was dedicated to his friend, singer Jeff Bucley.

2001 - He formed Audioslave with Tom Morello, Tim Commerford y Brad Wilk, ex-members of Rage Against the Machine.

2004 - He divorces Susan Silver, that same year he marries Karayannis.

2005 - Together with Audioslave, he offered a historic show in Havana, Cuba, which was one of the first openings on the island for a show by an American rock band.

2007 - He decides to leave Audioslave. he releases his second solo album "Carry On".

2009 - He releases his third solo album titled "Scream".

2010 - He collaborates with artists like slash and santana. He also participates in the Soundgarden reunion after 12 years of separation.

2015 - He releases his 5th solo studio album called Higher Truth, In addition to this, he restarted his tour with SoundGarden, which was left unfinished at the time of his death.

2017 - He died on May 18 at the age of 52 in Detroit.
